Let's help flood affected people in Bangladesh

Heavy monsoon rains over the past few days have led to rivers bursting their banks, cutting off around 2 million people in northeast Bangladesh. Many families are stranded and could run out of food within days.Homes and schools as well as vital infrastructure such as power supply lines, water sources and roads are all damaged or underwater. Many people have been affected by floods for the second time in a month, after severe flooding also struck the region in mid-May.

    Food for Families

    Marooned by flooding, thousands of residents of Sylhet are now facing a food and drinking water shortage.

    “I have never seen such a flood before. Most areas of the city are inundated. Water is entering people’s homes. Many have been without any food for days.”

    Mahbubur Rahman of Sadarpara, Sylhet

    Shelter for families

    Millions in Sylhet and Sunamganj now homeless and without any belongings.

    “The situation in Sylhet is dire. We do not have enough food or drinking water. All our belongings are underwater. We are spending our days sitting on the bed.”

    Sanjida Islam, a resident of the city’s Taltola area.

    Education for children

    Educational institutions have been closed in the affected areas.

    "A total of 290 primary schools, high schools, and colleges in Sylhet were closed due to the flooding."

    Abu Sayeed Md Wadud, District Education Officer, Sylhet.
